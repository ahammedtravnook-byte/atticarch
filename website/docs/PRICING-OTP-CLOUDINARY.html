SMS & Cloudinary Storage — Pricing

Verified against official Google Cloud (Firebase) and Cloudinary pricing pages, June 2026. USD→INR converted at ~₹85 per $1 (check the live rate when paying).

1. SMS — cost per message

Charged by Firebase Phone Authentication (Google Cloud Identity Platform) per SMS actually sent.

Destination of the phone number Price per SMS
India (your main audience) $0.01 ≈ ₹0.85
USA & Canada $0.01 ≈ ₹0.85
United Kingdom ~$0.04 ≈ ₹3.4
Most other countries (NRI enquiries) $0.02 – $0.06
India = about ₹0.85 per OTP message. The first 10 SMS per day are free. Billing only happens when the SMS is successfully sent.

Example: 1,000 OTP messages to India in a month ≈ $10 (≈ ₹850) before the free daily allowance is deducted. Rates are set by Google and can change; this is the current published rate.

2. Cloudinary — cost to increase storage

Cloudinary does not sell storage separately — storage is part of a monthly “credit” pool. 1 credit = 1 GB of storage. To get more storage you move up a plan.

Plan Price Credits / month Storage you can hold*
Free (current) $0 25 up to ~25 GB
Plus $99 / mo
($89/mo if paid yearly)		 225 up to ~225 GB
Advanced $249 / mo
($224/mo if paid yearly)		 600 up to ~600 GB

* The credit pool is shared between storage, visitor bandwidth and image transformations (1 credit = 1 GB storage or 1 GB bandwidth or 1,000 transformations). So real usable storage is whatever is left after bandwidth and transformations are used — the “up to” figures assume credits spent on storage only.

To increase storage, the only step up from Free is Plus at $99/month (≈ ₹8,400), which raises you from 25 GB to 225 GB. There is no cheaper in-between plan, and storage cannot be bought on its own — the next tier above Plus is Advanced at $249/month for 600 GB.

A portfolio site like ATTICARCH usually stores only 1–3 GB of project photos, so the Free 25 GB is plenty for storage today — you would only need Plus if total usage (storage + traffic bandwidth) crosses 25 credits in a month.
